# Cable Management

LAN:
RJ45: Cat6, Cat7
#
Internet:
Ethernet (copper) or Fiber

*if unavailable or opting for a second WAN then cell, sat, and dsl is okay*
#

All cable should be bound by an easily removable restraint that does not damage or overly bend the cable:
- Removable so maintenance and adjustment can occur.
- Reastraint so cable appears professional and kept. 
- We avoid damaging or bending the cable to prevent signal loss and uncessary extra maintenance.

**Examples:** Velcro, Twist Ties - Clean and Effective.
**Bad Examples:** 
- Tape can damage the cable
- Zip ties are not easily removable
- String, dangles and gives an unprofessional or ugly look. 
#
No cable should directly plug into infrastructure. Both the Core and Switch should have a patch panel with short jumper cables to feed the equipment. 

No cable should be exposed or hanging outside of designated communications rooms or datacenters, all drops should connect to an ethernet wall plate.

If cables cannot be managed via wall plates, then cable runs via PVC, or runways can be installed. 
#

Excess cable must be looped and managed by cable management, do not leave hanging. 
#
Cable must not touch the ground, it will be stepped on. 
#
